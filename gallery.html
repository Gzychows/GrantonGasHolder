The Granton Gas Holder is a historic landmark located in the Granton area of Edinburgh, Scotland. It was built in 1903 and was used to store gas for the city's streetlights and homes. The structure is 91 meters in height and was one of the largest in Europe when it was built. It was decommissioned in 1997 but still stands today as a landmark and a reminder of the city's industrial past.
